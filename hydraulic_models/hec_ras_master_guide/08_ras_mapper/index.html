    🗺️ Chapter 8: RAS Mapper & Spatial Visualisation

    💡 Pro Tip: A mathematically perfect hydraulic model is useless if you cannot communicate the risk to non-engineers, urban planners, or emergency responders. RAS Mapper bridges the gap between raw computational matrices and actionable, visual intelligence.

    HEC-RAS Mapper is the integrated Geographic Information System (GIS) environment built directly into the software. Introduced in Version 5.0, it revolutionised hydraulic engineering by eliminating the heavy reliance on external GIS tools for mesh generation and result visualisation. It reads the massive binary Results files (.hdf) generated by your simulations and translates them into dynamic spatial maps.

    A. Terrain Import & Management (The Foundation)

    You cannot view spatial results without a terrain base. HEC-RAS requires you to build a "RAS Terrain" before you can map flood extents.

    1. The HDF Conversion Process

    When you download a raw GeoTIFF DEM from USGS Earth Explorer or Bhuvan ISRO, HEC-RAS does not read it directly during computation. * How to Import: Open RAS Mapper. Right-click the Terrains layer group $\rightarrow$ Create a New RAS Terrain. Click the plus icon to add your .tif files. * What Happens: The software converts your GeoTIFF into a proprietary, highly compressed Hierarchical Data Format (.hdf) file. It builds multiple "pyramid" levels of resolution, which allows you to zoom in and out of a gigabyte-sized terrain instantly without crashing your graphics card.

    2. Terrain Stitching (Mosaicking)

    If your study area crosses multiple downloaded DEM tiles, you do not necessarily need to stitch them in QGIS beforehand. * The HEC-RAS Method: You can load multiple .tif files into the "Create New RAS Terrain" window simultaneously. The software will stitch them together. You can even assign priorities (e.g., placing a high-resolution 1m LiDAR survey mathematically "on top" of a 30m regional satellite DEM).

    B. Generating and Interpreting Flood Maps

    By default, HEC-RAS computes the physics but does not draw the visual maps to save hard drive space. You must manually generate the map layers you want to see.

    1. Activating Results

    1. Under the Results node in the layer tree, expand the specific Flow Plan you just ran.
    2. Right-click the plan name $\rightarrow$ Create a New Results Map Layer.

    2. Water Surface Elevation (WSE) vs. Depth

    It is critical to understand the engineering difference between these two primary maps: * Water Surface Elevation (WSE): Maps the absolute elevation of the floodwater above mean sea level. * Use Case: You use WSE maps to compare against the surveyed crest elevation of a levee or the low chord (bottom) of a bridge deck to see if it will overtop. * Depth: Maps how deep the water is relative to the ground immediately below it ($Depth = WSE - Terrain$). * Use Case: This is the most critical map for public safety, damage curves, and emergency response. (e.g., "The water inside this residential neighbourhood is exactly 1.5 meters deep").

    3. Velocity & Shear Stress Maps

    • Velocity: Critical for assessing human safety and evacuation routes. Water that is only 0.5 meters deep but moving at $3 \text{ m/s}$ possesses enough momentum to sweep a vehicle off a paved road.
    • Shear Stress: Shows the erosive frictional force the water exerts on the channel bed. High shear stress zones indicate areas where severe soil erosion or bridge pier scour will occur during the flood.

    C. Dynamic Animation & Particle Tracing

    Static maps only tell half the story. Unsteady flow models allow you to visualise the hydrodynamics over time.

    1. Animating the Flood Wave

    If you ran an Unsteady simulation, a time-slider bar appears at the top right of the RAS Mapper interface. * Click Play to watch the hydrograph route through your river system. * You can visually verify if water pools behind a highway embankment before eventually spilling over the top, proving that your breaklines are functioning correctly.

    2. Particle Tracing (Visualising Hydrodynamics)

    In a 2D model, water does not just flow straight; it swirls, eddies, and backflows. * How to Activate: Right-click your active Velocity map layer and select Particle Tracing. * The Result: RAS Mapper generates animated tracer particles that flow with the water currents. * Engineering Value: This is an incredible diagnostic tool. It explicitly shows where dangerous eddy currents form around bridge abutments or where flow stagnates entirely in ineffective flow areas.

    D. GIS Export & Professional Cartography

    While RAS Mapper is an exceptional diagnostic tool, it lacks the layout features needed for a legally submissible engineering report (like title blocks, north arrows, and scale bars).

    Exporting for Final Cartography

    1. In the Layer Tree, right-click the specific map you want to export (e.g., Depth at the absolute maximum water surface).
    2. Select Export Layer $\rightarrow$ Export to GeoTIFF.
    3. Drag this exported .tif file into a dedicated GIS platform like QGIS or ArcGIS Pro.
    4. In the external GIS, you can overlay the flood map onto high-resolution satellite imagery, clip it to municipal boundaries, and format a professional map sheet for stakeholders.
